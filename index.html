CVA Drivers — EE, EPE, PD & EAD per Counterparty

▶ Simulation Methodology
  • Engine: 10,000-path Monte Carlo using log-normal diffusion for FX rates (GBM) and Hull-White 1-factor for IR. Correlation structure preserved via Cholesky decomposition of historical 1Y covariance matrix.
  • Market scenarios: Each path draws risk factor shocks from a calibrated covariance matrix. Risk factors: USD 6M SOFR, EUR 6M EURIBOR, EUR/USD spot, USD/GBP spot, equity index returns.
  • EE computation: At each time step t, EE(t) = E[max(V(t), 0)] averaged across all 10,000 paths under the risk-neutral measure, where V(t) is the NPV of the netting set.
  • PFE computation: PFE(t, 95%) = 95th percentile of the simulated exposure distribution at each time step t. PFE is NOT the average — it is a quantile and represents the exposure level breached by only 5% of paths.
  • EPE: EPE = (1/T) × Σ EE(tᵢ) × Δtᵢ — the time-weighted average of the EE profile. EPE is the key input to CVA: CVA = LGD × Σ EPE(tᵢ) × PD(tᵢ) × DF(tᵢ).
  • Netting: Exposure computed at netting set level. Net MtM = max(Σ Vᵢ, 0) — negative-value trades reduce exposure within the same ISDA master agreement. Netting benefit across this portfolio: ~38%.
  • Limitation: Model assumes normal correlation structure which breaks down under stress. Jump-to-default scenarios and WWR require a separate correlated default simulation. Current model uses static correlation — a dynamic correlation model (e.g. DCC-GARCH) would be more robust.

▶ VaR Methodology — Formulas & Assumptions
  • Parametric VaR: VaR(α, T) = −(μ·T − zα·σ·√T·W) where zα is the inverse normal CDF at confidence level α, σ is daily portfolio vol, W is portfolio value, T is horizon in days. At 99% confidence: z = 2.326.
  • 10-Day scaling: VaR10D = VaR1D × √10 — the square-root-of-time rule. Valid only under i.i.d. normally distributed returns. For autocorrelated or fat-tailed returns, this understates risk. Basel III mandates this scaling for regulatory capital purposes.
  • CVaR (Expected Shortfall): CVaR(α) = −E[L | L > VaR(α)] = (1/(1−α)) × ∫VaR(α)∞ l·f(l) dl. Under normality: CVaR(α) = μ + σ·φ(zα)/(1−α) where φ is the standard normal PDF. CVaR is a coherent risk measure (satisfies sub-additivity) — VaR is not.
  • Diversification: Portfolio VaR = √(w'Σw) × zα × √T where Σ is the covariance matrix of daily P&L. Sum-of-individual-VaR assumes perfect correlation — the difference is the diversification benefit ($23.5M at 99% 1D).
  • Key limitation — normality: Credit spread returns are negatively skewed with excess kurtosis ≈ 4–6 (normal = 0). This makes parametric VaR systematically understate tail losses. Historical simulation is more robust but sensitive to the lookback window (250d excludes pre-2023 stress).
▶ VaR vs PFE — The CCR Distinction
  • VaR is a market risk metric. It measures short-horizon (1D, 10D) loss on a current portfolio assuming positions are static. It answers: "what can go wrong tomorrow?"
  • PFE is a credit risk metric. It measures long-horizon (months to years) potential exposure to a counterparty default, accounting for trade evolution under Monte Carlo simulation. It answers: "if the counterparty defaults in year 3, what do we lose?"
  • Why both are needed: VaR captures the P&L volatility of the CVA desk hedging portfolio (CDS positions, rate swaps). PFE captures the structural credit exposure that determines CVA capital. They are complementary — a bank managing a CVA desk runs VaR on the hedge book and PFE on the underlying derivative portfolio.
  • FRTB alignment: Under FRTB (2026), CVA capital is measured via the CVA IMA which uses an Expected Shortfall (CVaR) measure with a 97.5% confidence level over a 1-year horizon — bridging VaR methodology into CCR capital. This is the regulatory point where market risk and credit risk measurement formally converge.
  • WWR amplification of tail: Under wrong-way risk, exposure and default probability co-move. This creates a joint tail event that VaR cannot capture (VaR is unconditional) but that CVaR begins to approximate through the tail conditioning. Full WWR requires a copula-based joint simulation.

1. SA-CCR — Standardised Approach to CCR
EAD = α × (RC + AddOnagg)
α = 1.4 (regulatory multiplier)
RC = max(V − C, TH + MTA − NICA, 0)
AddOn = Σasset class AddOnj
AddOntrade = δ × d × MF × SF × AdjNotional

Assumption: Static supervisory factors — do not change with market conditions. MF assumes uniform maturity distribution within bucket. Limitation: Overestimates EAD by ~15–35% vs IMM for typical IRS portfolios. Does not capture netting benefit across asset classes.

2. CVA — Credit Valuation Adjustment
CVA = LGD × Σᵢ EE*(tᵢ) × PD(tᵢ₋₁, tᵢ) × DF(tᵢ)
EE*(t) = E[max(V(t), 0)] (risk-neutral)
PD(t) = 1 − S(t)
S(t) = exp(−λt) λ = CDS/(1−R)
LGD = 1 − Recovery
DF(t) = exp(−r·t) r = risk-free rate

Assumption: Independence of exposure and default (no WWR). Constant hazard rate (flat CDS curve). LGD is deterministic. Limitation: WWR correlation inflates CVA by up to 34% (see WWR tab). Flat CDS curve assumption understates long-dated CVA when the credit curve is upward sloping.

3. PFE & EE — Exposure Metrics
EE(t) = E[max(V(t), 0)]
PFE(t, α) = quantile_α[max(V(t), 0)]
EPE = (1/T) × Σᵢ EE(tᵢ) × Δtᵢ
EEPE = max(EPE, EE(0))

IR: drt = κ(θ−rt)dt + σ dWt (Hull-White)
FX: dSt = μ·St·dt + σ·St·dWt (GBM)
Corr: ρ via Cholesky decomp of Σ

Assumption: Hull-White 1F for IR (mean reversion, single vol parameter). GBM for FX (log-normal). 10,000 paths, monthly time steps. Limitation: HW1F cannot capture volatility smile. GBM assumes log-normal FX — underestimates tail jumps. Static correlation matrix breaks under stress.

4. VaR & CVaR — Tail Risk Measures
VaR(α,T) = −(μT − z_α·σ·√T·W)
z_99% = 2.326 z_95% = 1.645
VaR_10D = VaR_1D × √10 (i.i.d. returns)

CVaR(α) = E[L | L > VaR(α)]
= μ + σ·φ(z_α)/(1−α) [parametric]

PortVaR = √(w'Σw) × z_α × √T × W

Assumption: Parametric: normally distributed P&L (violated for credit). Historical: past distribution is representative. Limitation: Normal VaR underestimates tail by ~22% for this portfolio (credit fat tails). √10 scaling assumes returns are i.i.d. — autocorrelation in spread moves violates this. CVaR is model-sensitive in the tail.

5. XVA — Full Valuation Adjustment Stack
CVA = LGD_B × Σ EE*(t)·PD_B(t)·DF(t)
DVA = LGD_A × Σ NEE*(t)·PD_A(t)·DF(t)
BCVA = CVA − DVA
FVA = funding_spread × Σ EFNI(t)·DF(t)
KVA = CoC × Σ EC(t)·DF(t)

Adjusted FV = MtM − CVA + DVA − FVA − KVA
WWR-Adj CVA = CVA × (1 + ρ·amplifier)

Assumption: DVA computed symmetrically to CVA using own CDS spread. FVA uses OIS-funded assumption. KVA uses 12% cost of capital. Limitation: DVA monetisation requires own-name CDS buyback — not always feasible. FVA and DVA double-count in some frameworks. KVA is model-dependent on capital projection method.

6. RAROC — Risk-Adjusted Return on Capital
RAROC = Net Revenue / Economic Capital
Net Revenue = Fees − CVA − FVA − KVA − Funding
EC = UL = VaR(99.9%, 1Y) − EL
EL = EAD × PD × LGD
Hurdle Rate = 10–12% (typical bank target)
Decision: RAROC > hurdle → value-creating trade

Assumption: Economic capital is the 99.9% 1Y VaR of the credit loss distribution. Net revenue includes all XVA charges. Limitation: EC is model-sensitive (copula, PD calibration). A RAROC above hurdle does not mean the trade is safe — only that it generates sufficient return per unit of capital.

7. PD & Survival Probability
Hazard rate: λ = CDS_spread / (1 − R)
Survival: S(T) = exp(−λ·T)
PD(T) = 1 − S(T)
Marginal PD(t,t+Δt) = S(t) − S(t+Δt)

Recovery by jurisdiction:
EU BRRD (bail-in): R ≈ 30–38%
US Chapter 11 (G-SIB): R ≈ 40–45%

Assumption: Constant hazard rate (flat CDS term structure). Recovery rate is known and deterministic at default. Limitation: In practice, recovery is stochastic and negatively correlated with default frequency (lower recovery in systemic crises). A random recovery model (e.g., Beta distribution) is more accurate for tail scenarios.
